Understanding Mental Health

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices.

Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood. Good mental health is more than just the absence of mental illness. It means you are in a state of well-being in which you feel good and function well in the world.

There are many factors that can affect mental health, including genetics, brain chemistry, trauma, and life experiences. Taking care of your mental health is just as important as taking care of your physical health.
